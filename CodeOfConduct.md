## Code of Conduct
##### Ryan "NvKopres" Almond, and Rakyuu Studio.
##### Verified on Feb/2024/17, Language: en-US (English)
***
This code of conduct outlines me (or even my studio) expectations for all warm-hearted
to help me (or us) to make the opensource project community better, as
well as all the consequences of unacceptable behavior.

We welcome all those who participate to help us create a safe and happy experience
for everyone. And to make this Code of Conduct, to make the community or even the
society more positive and make sure forbid abuse the power.

***

### What to do
***
- **Respect**. Respect for others is the most basic rule. You need to respect all the developers who contribute in this open source community. It's fine to express your disagreement to others, but insult or slander those developers are not. Please be responsible for every word you say.
- **Participate and Collaboration**.
  We welcome everyone to participate in this open source project, regardless of your level of experience or personal characteristics. In order for everyone to work together, we need to follow the guidelines below:
    1. Report Issues:  
       When you report a problem or issues, please provide details as much as possible that we can understand your issues better.  
       To avoid duplicating issues that have been reported, please search the issue list, or ask the moderators in the community.
    2. Contribute Code:  
       Please be respectful of others and avoid using offensive language.Try to share your opinion, but be open to disagreements. Please make sure that what you say is relevant to the topic, and use correct grammar and spelling where possible.  
       Before submitting your code, make sure you have tested your code to make sure it works.
       Follow the project's coding specifications and make sure your code conforms to best practices.
       When you submit your code, provide detailed instructions so that others can understand your changes.
    3. Be Active:
       Participate in community events such as online gatherings, seminars, and conferences to foster collaboration and communication.
       **But at least: Be clear about your expectations and ask for help, and be willing to help others.**
***
### What NOT to do
***
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind, including post Sex Image or Sex Text.
- Public or Private harassment including posting home address or other personal information.
- Trolling, insulting, or even derogatory comments, and personality or political comments.
***
### Punishments
***
If you violate any of the "What NOT to do" sections of this Code of Conduct, you will be warned once for the first offense and demoted for one day if you are a Moderator; for the second offense, you will be punished depending on the severity of the situation, including but not limited to banning, dismissal, or expulsion; for the third offense, you will be expelled from the community.
